# Commercial Licensing

loop is free software under the [GNU Affero General Public License, version 3 or
later](./LICENSE) (AGPL-3.0-or-later). For most open-source and personal use, the
AGPL is all you need, and it costs nothing.

This page is for the case where the AGPL's terms **don't** fit.

## When you need a commercial license

The AGPL is strong copyleft with a network clause. If you **modify loop and run it
to provide a service over a network** (a hosted remembrance or interview service,
say), the AGPL requires you to offer the complete corresponding source of your
modified version to that service's users. Redistributing loop as part of a larger
work carries the same obligation. A commercial license may fit if you want to:

- **Embed loop in a closed-source product** you distribute, without releasing your
  own source under the AGPL.
- **Run a modified loop as a hosted network service** without the AGPL's
  requirement to publish your modifications to users.

If you're using stock loop yourself, building open-source software, or contributing
back, you almost certainly do **not** need a commercial license.

## What a commercial license grants

A commercial license grants **relief from the AGPL's copyleft and
network-disclosure obligations** for the agreed scope. It does **not** by itself
include a warranty, support, indemnity, or an SLA — those can be negotiated
separately but are not implied. loop's technical claim is unchanged by any
license: tamper-**evidence** on one machine, never tamper-proof. A commercial
license changes your **licensing** obligations, not what the software guarantees.

**Note — loop runs on [Rusty](https://github.com/TheLakeMan/rusty)**, which is
itself AGPL-3.0-or-later and dual-licensed by the same author. A commercial
deployment that embeds Rusty can be licensed together — mention your setup in the
inquiry and we'll cover both.
